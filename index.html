About Me

Tzipi Shavit (Hebrew: ציפי שביט; born April 1, 1947) is an Israeli actress, comedian entertainer, who is best known as a children's entertainer. She is sometimes described by the media as the "First Lady of Israeli entertainment."[1]
Shavit was born in kibbutz Neve Yam, Israel. She served in the Israel Defense Forces in Tzevet Havai of the Southern Command.
Shortly after that, she participated in the film Our Neighborhood. In 1969 her manager got her a part in the stage comedy show A Funny Thing Happened to Me On The Way To Suez written by Saul Biber The show was canceled shortly thereafter due to a publicized objection by a bereaved mother who lost her son in The Sinai War. In 1971, Shavit played in the stage musical Jumbo in which she sang the song "Everyone went to the Jumbo" to the tune of the Italian song "Volevo un gatto nero". In 1972, Shavit acted in the stage show Chocolate Mint Gum - The Zionist Congress of laughter alongside Yardena Arazi. In the Spring of 2016, performed in the play "Noah's Ark."[2] In 2017, she starred in the play My Mother the General (Hebrew: אמי הגנרלית) an adaptation from the 1979 film by the same name.[3] Shavit was a featured singer in the song and music video "Casanova (Good Aunt)" by Israeli singers Eve and Lear[4] (Hebrew: איב אנד ליר).[5]

The first generation of the World Wide Web, which was characterized by static web pages with basic HTML formatting, limited interactivity, and a one-way flow of information from website owners to website visitors. Websites were mostly informational in nature and were designed for individual consumption rather than collaboration. The second generation of the World Wide Web, which emerged in the late 1990s and early 2000s. This era of the internet was marked by the rise of user-generated content, social networking, and collaboration among users. Web2.0 technologies made it possible for users to interact with each other, share information, and create content. Examples of Web2.0 platforms include social media sites like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram, as well as collaborative tools like wikis and blogs. The current stage of the evolution of the internet, which is still in its early stages. It is often referred to as the "Semantic Web" and is characterized by the use of artificial intelligence, blockchain technology, and decentralized systems to create a more intelligent and secure web. Web3 aims to make the internet more decentralized, democratized, and secure by giving users more control over their data and online identities. Examples of Web3 technologies include decentralized apps (dApps), blockchain-based platforms, and decentralized finance (DeFi) systems.
